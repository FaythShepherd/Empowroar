Device Security

How to keep your device safe


5 Quick Tips

How can you secure your device? Why is it important?

Our phones and other devices are amazing tools that help us live in todays fast paced world. Our devices contain a lot of personal data that if stolen could give others access to our personal bank accounts, emails, work emails, social media account and more. This can lead to you identity being stolen. It is so important that we understand what kind of information our devices store about us and how to protect it.



Phyically Protect it!


Don't leave your phone, laptop, or tablet out in public, especially where anyone could walk by and take it.

Use Two Factor Authentication


Two factor authentication means that when you login to a account, it has you verify your identiy by entering a code sent to your phone number or email.

Use Biometric Authentication


This is like two factor authentication, but it uses your Face ID or fingerprint to verify identity.

Never Share Your Passwords


This should be obvious, however - NEVER give our your password! You can't trust what someone else will do with it.


Keep Your Device Up To Date


Keep your device running on the latest operating system, this will keep your device protected and make it more difficult to hack.

Be Suspicious


Avoid clicking on links that look suspicious. Hackers will try to trick you with authentic looking fakes. If you get a strange text or email from your bank or other account, don't be afraid to call them and ask about it.

Back-Up Your Device


Have your device backed up, for an Apple device you can use iCloud or for Android you can use Google One.

Use a VPN


A VPN is a service that protects your personal data while you are on a public Wi-Fi. Some great ones are NordVPN, ProtonVPN, and Atlas.

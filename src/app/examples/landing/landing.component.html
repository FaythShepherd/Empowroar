Let's talk about Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying is bullying that takes place over digital devices like cell phones, computers, and tablets. Cyberbullying can occur through SMS, Text, and apps, or online in social media, forums, or gaming where people can view, participate in, or share content. Cyberbullying includes sending, posting, or sharing negative, harmful, false, or mean content about someone else. It can include sharing personal or private information about someone else causing embarrassment or humiliation. Some cyberbullying crosses the line into unlawful or criminal behavior.

How to Heal from Cyberbullying

Talk About It

Talk to a trusted adult about how you are feelings, show them the hateful posts/messages. Ask for help.

Seek Therapy

Working with a Therapist is a wonderful way to get help. Healing starts with a look on the inside.

Time Away

Take time away from social media and other online communication platforms. Block people who intend to harm you.

Love over Hate

Surround youself with people who love you! Daily spend time looking into the mirror and say one thing you love about yourself to yourself.



Hobbies

Replace your online time with a loved hobby of yours or try something new! Try learning something new!

Writing

Writing your feelings is a great way to heal. Be brave enough to create a list of all the wonderful things about you.

Exercise

Whether it is a 30 minute walk with a friend, pickleball with the fam or even iceskating! Physically care for your body.

The Great Outdoors

Watch a sunrise or sunset, take moment to look at the sky, trees, flowers. Connect with the physical world.

Other Reasources

Stomp Out Bullying

Another great site dedicated to ending hate

Stomp Out Bullying is a wonderful tool for kids, teens, parents and teachers! It offers a crisis hotline, other media and material to help change the culture and end the hate. There are ways to donate and get your school involved.

Stop Bullying

A federal initiative to stop bullying

Even the nation recongizes this as an important issue. This site contains AMAZING material to help all ages understand what is bullying how to stop yourself from doing and helping victims. It also puts you in touch with help hotlines.

Kids Help Line

A online tool helping kids

This wonderful site is kid friendly, with wonderful graphics and sections dedicated to educating and helping specific ages groups. Great tool for parents and kids to explore together. Includes a help hotline and selection of apps for therapy, mood trackers, etc.
